Portfolio

As a professional in an ever-changing labour market with limitless possibilities and opportunity around every corner, it is important for you to be prepared to articulate and demonstrate your skills and experience. You need to know yourself and what you bring to positions.

The portfolio development process will help facilitate intentional self-reflection and discovery. The goal of a Portfolio is to help you capture, organize, and maintain a record of your learning, experience, skills, and development. It is a career development tool you can easily access, update and review throughout your employment and academic journey. 

Checklists

Present your portfolio in one of two ways: a LinkedIn Profile or website. You may also choose to do both and link them to one another. Whichever platform you choose, your Portfolio should be professionally presented and represent your personal ‘brand’.  In keeping with NSCC’s approach to portfolio learning, students will follow the check list below and ensure their portfolio is detailed and professional.

Timeline

  • First Term
    • Review all of the Portfolio information contained on this site to ensure you understand the requirements
    • Create a 'Portfolio' File in your One Drive – this is where you will keep everything you want to include in your Portfolio
    • TIP!! Download assignments from Brightspace before the course closes – you will lose access to this information
  • Each Term
    • Choose one or two samples of your work/'evidence' of your skills for each course
    • Choose which platform you will use for your Portfolio
    • Begin process of building and organizing your portfolio.
    • TIP!! Faculty may have indicated which assignments are 'Portfolio Friendly' - if not, ask!
  • Final Term
    • Add final touches
    • Proofread
    • Organize - add table of contents
    • Compile your Learning Narrative
    • TIP!! Review the Library Subject Guide on Learning Narratives for guidance
  • Portfolio Review Appointment
    • Your Faculty for Work Experience will be assessing your portfolio
    • Prior to requesting a portfolio appointment, review the information and instructions you have been provided
    • Include your name, student (w) #, and 'Portfolio Review' in the subject line of the email requesting a Portfolio review appointment
    • Review with a friend or another student before your appointment! TIP!! Use the 'checklist' to confirm you have everything included before your Portfolio review
